# Video Form Analysis Guide

## Overview
The Video Form Analysis feature uses advanced computer vision technology to evaluate your exercise form in real-time. This guide will help you understand how to use this feature effectively to improve your workouts and prevent injuries.

## Supported Exercises
Currently, the system analyzes two fundamental exercises:
- **Squat**: Analyzes depth, knee tracking, back angle, and balance
- **Deadlift**: Evaluates bar path, hip hinge, back angle, and lockout position

More exercises will be added in future updates.

## Getting Started

### Prerequisites
- A device with a front-facing camera
- Good lighting conditions
- Space to perform exercises safely
- Stable internet connection (for processing)

### Setup Process
1. Navigate to the "Video Analysis" section in the main menu
2. Allow camera access when prompted by your browser
3. Position your device at a suitable height and distance
4. Ensure your full body is visible in the frame
5. Select the exercise you want to analyze

## Using the Squat Analysis

### Preparation
1. Stand in front of your device with the camera at chest height
2. Ensure the floor surface is visible and well-lit
3. Wear form-fitting clothing for better joint detection
4. Clear a space large enough to perform squats safely

### Performing the Analysis
1. Select "Squat" from the exercise options
2. Position yourself in the center of the frame
3. Wait for the system to detect your pose
4. Begin performing squats at a controlled pace
5. Watch the real-time feedback on your form quality

### Understanding Squat Metrics
- **Depth**: Measures how deep you go (aim for hip crease below parallel)
- **Knee Tracking**: Checks if knees stay aligned with toes
- **Back Angle**: Monitors forward lean (maintain neutral spine)
- **Balance**: Evaluates weight distribution (keep heels grounded)

### Common Squat Corrections
- **Too Shallow**: Lower your hips until thighs are at least parallel to the floor
- **Knee Valgus**: Focus on pushing knees outward, in line with toes
- **Forward Lean**: Engage core, keep chest up and shoulders back
- **Heel Lift**: Distribute weight evenly across your entire foot

## Using the Deadlift Analysis

### Preparation
1. Position your device perpendicular to your movement path
2. Place a barbell or similar object in your frame for reference
3. Ensure good lighting so the system can track the bar path
4. Have enough space to perform deadlifts safely

### Performing the Analysis
1. Select "Deadlift" from the exercise options
2. Position the barbell over mid-foot in the frame
3. Set up in the starting position
4. Perform the lift while maintaining proper form
5. Observe the real-time form feedback

### Understanding Deadlift Metrics
- **Bar Path**: Tracks if the bar stays close to the body
- **Hip Hinge**: Checks proper hip movement initiation
- **Back Angle**: Monitors spine position throughout the lift
- **Lockout**: Evaluates complete extension at the top

### Common Deadlift Corrections
- **Bar Drift**: Keep the bar close to your body throughout the movement
- **Early Hip Rise**: Initiate the lift with hips and shoulders moving together
- **Rounded Back**: Maintain neutral spine, engage core muscles
- **Incomplete Lockout**: Fully extend hips and knees at the top of the movement

## Interpreting Results

### Form Score
- Scores range from 0 to 100
- Higher scores indicate better form
- Focus on gradual improvement rather than perfect scores
- Consistency matters more than individual high scores

### Feedback Categories
- **Excellent (85-100)**: Great form, minor optimizations possible
- **Good (70-84)**: Solid technique, few areas for improvement
- **Fair (55-69)**: Several form issues to address
- **Needs Work (0-54)**: Significant corrections needed

### Improvement Tips
1. Focus on one aspect of your form at a time
2. Practice movements slowly before increasing speed
3. Record yourself to compare with ideal form examples
4. Use the coaching recommendations for targeted improvements
5. Track your scores over time to measure progress

## Best Practices

### Environment Setup
- Use in a well-lit room (natural light preferred)
- Ensure a clutter-free background
- Position camera at appropriate height for the exercise
- Maintain consistent distance from camera during exercise

### Clothing Recommendations
- Wear form-fitting clothes in contrasting colors to background
- Avoid loose clothing that obscures body lines
- Choose solid colors over busy patterns
- Ensure joints (knees, elbows, shoulders) are visible

### Movement Guidelines
- Perform movements at a controlled pace
- Allow the system time to process each repetition
- Stay centered in the frame throughout the movement
- Minimize extraneous movements during analysis

## Limitations and Considerations

### Known Limitations
- Accuracy may vary in low-light conditions
- Multiple people in frame may confuse detection
- Fast movements may reduce accuracy
- Extreme angles may affect joint tracking

### Safety Considerations
- Always prioritize safety over getting perfect form scores
- Use appropriate weights for your ability level
- Stop immediately if you feel pain or discomfort
- Have adequate space to perform exercises safely

### Privacy Information
- Video feed is processed locally when possible
- No video recordings are stored without explicit permission
- Data is encrypted and stored securely
- You control what data is shared and retained

## Troubleshooting

### Common Issues
- **Poor Detection**: Ensure good lighting and wear contrasting clothing
- **Camera Not Working**: Check browser permissions and refresh the page
- **Slow Response**: Close other browser tabs and applications
- **Incorrect Feedback**: Verify your positioning in the frame

### Improving Accuracy
- Ensure consistent lighting throughout your session
- Keep your entire body in the frame at all times
- Perform movements at a moderate pace
- Calibrate your setup with known good form first

## Advanced Features

### Historical Tracking
- View your form scores over time
- Compare current performance to previous sessions
- Identify trends in your technique
- Set improvement goals based on data

### Trend Analysis
- Monitor your progress week over week
- Identify which aspects of your form improve fastest
- Track consistency in your technique
